Multi-modal Multi-channel Speech Separation

Authors: Rongzhi Gu, Shi-Xiong Zhang, Lianwu Chen, Yong Xu, Yuexian Zou, Dong Yu

Abstract: Target speech separation refers to extracting the target speaker's voice from the multi-speaker speech mixture. Recently, multi-modal speaker separation has attracted increasing focus, owing to the benefits brought by jointly modeling of visual and audio features. Previous methods concentrate on monaural speech separation in a controlled acoustic environment. In this work, we tackle the target speech separation as a real-world problem, namely arbitrary number of unknown mixed speakers, noise and reverberation, videos in the wild. To deal with the challenging scenario, we propose a deep multi-modal framework that fully exploits the target-related information, including the direction, lip movements and voice characteristics of the target speaker. Meanwhile, we investigate on efficient and robust multi-modality fusion approaches within the framework. A factorized attention fusion method is proposed to effectively aggregate multiple modalities. It factorizes the audio modality into a set of acoustic subspaces and utilizes information from other modalities to aggregate them with a learnable attention scheme. Furthermore, we verify the robustness of proposed multi-modal model when one of the modality is temporarily missing, invalid or corrupted. Experiments are carried out on a simulated audio-visual Mandarin dataset collected from YouTube website. Evaluation results demonstrate the efficiency and robustness of our proposed multi-modal model.

Dataset: Mandarin-mix
